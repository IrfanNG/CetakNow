# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are small print shop owners and staff, especially shops near student areas or campuses. They currently handle orders through WhatsApp, walk-ins, or simple forms, and need a clearer way to receive files, confirm payment, organize pickup slots, and avoid unpaid printing work.

Secondary users are customers placing print orders through a shop-specific CetakNow link. They need a fast guest flow: upload PDF, choose print settings, pay first, and pick up at the selected slot.

## Product Purpose

CetakNow turns messy print-shop ordering into a structured paid online workflow. The MVP helps shops validate demand through a SaaS landing page, direct subscription checkout, self-service shop setup after payment, automatic shop-link generation, multi-tenant shop pages, PDF-only upload, automatic page/price calculation, online payment before printing, pickup slots, admin dashboards, notification logs, and 7-day file cleanup.

Success means shop owners understand the value quickly, trust the platform enough to submit interest, and can see how CetakNow reduces WhatsApp chaos, manual calculation, and unpaid/no-show print losses.

## Brand Personality

Local, trustworthy, efficient.

The voice is practical Bahasa Melayu first, direct, calm, and owner-friendly. It should feel like a reliable local operations tool, not a distant corporate SaaS or playful student experiment.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS template visuals, over-corporate fintech styling, childish student-project UI, excessive glassmorphism, repeated identical card grids, vague startup jargon, and English-first copy when the target owner context is local.

Avoid making `Log Masuk` compete with the acquisition CTAs. `Daftar` in the navbar and `Daftar Sekarang` in the final CTA route users to the Harga section first; admin login is secondary.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with order clarity**: show how files, payment, status, and pickup become organized.
2. **Trust before flash**: use clean hierarchy, concrete proof, and calm reassurance over decorative effects.
3. **BM-first local confidence**: write in natural Bahasa Melayu with English only where it matches real shop language.
4. **Payment-first discipline**: reinforce that paid orders reduce wasted printing and no-show risk.
5. **Mobile owner speed**: keep key actions reachable for busy shop owners checking from a phone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain visible focus states, readable contrast, 44px minimum touch targets, semantic headings, descriptive image alt text, keyboard-accessible forms, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Avoid communicating meaning by color alone.
